The Commute Game Plan: "Soletra" Edition

Every day, I take an Uber to work. My commute usually takes between 45 minutes and 1 hour and 15 minutes — it all depends on traffic.

To distract myself from the long commute, I start my day playing Soletra, a Brazilian version of Spelling Bee. The problem? I live in São Paulo, one of the Brazilian cities with the highest rates of cellphone theft.

Many of these crimes occur during traffic jams, when motorcyclists take advantage of the congestion to break car windows and steal from drivers stuck in traffic.

This is my daily route

The route starts in a green zone, where I feel relatively safe playing Soletra. Even though the risk is still present, it's the part of the commute where I’m most relaxed.

As I move along, I pass through several stretches marked in yellow. These are caution zones — I stay alert, especially when traffic slows down or comes to a stop, as those moments can quickly become dangerous.

At certain points, I enter areas highlighted in red. These are absolute no-go zones for taking out my phone. The risk of being robbed here is simply too high.

Robberies

Each point on this map represents one cellphone theft reported in 2025 up until April.

Inside vehicles

The red dots show robberies that happened while the victim was inside a vehicle.

Not far from reality

Based on the data provided by the city hall, the areas where I fear using my phone the most are also where more thefts occur.
